Branden-Krasikov-Shapiro Type Theorem for Linear Difference Operator

December, 2024

In this paper, we give a complete characterisation of the entire function f which both f and its general linear difference Lf omit zero. It can be seen as a difference version of the Frank-Hellerstein theorem, which can be regarded as a dual version of (localized) Hermite-Poulain theorem in some sense. With this characterisation in hand, we establish a version of Brändén-Krasikov-Shapiro theorem for linear difference operator preserving ΠE(∅). Our method can be extended partially to a meromorphic function with finitely many zeros and poles.
In collaboration with Xiao Yao and Yiming Zhang.

Long Memory in Stock Return Volatility in Chinese Stock Market

April, 2024

Graduation thesis, under the supervision of Professor Mingtao Wang.
This paper investigates long memory in the volatility of daily squared returns for Chinese stocks using GPH estimation. Rolling estimation reveals that 98.92% of stocks show significant long memory. A cross-sectional analysis finds a negative correlation between long memory and future excess returns, suggesting that higher volatility predictability leads to lower risk premiums. The findings are robust across industry sectors.
